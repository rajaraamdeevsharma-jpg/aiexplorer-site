What to expect

Your child's first 6 weeks.

A structured, fun journey from zero to building real AI.

Week 1–2
What Is AI? The Basics
Students identify AI in everyday life, play the "Be the AI" sorting game, and build their first image classifier using Google Teachable Machine.
Week 3–4
How AI Learns From Data
Students collect their own data, train a model, and discover what happens when training data is unfair — a hands-on intro to AI bias.
Week 5
Build Your Own Chatbot
Using Scratch or simple Python blocks, students design a conversational agent for a task they choose — greeting visitors, telling jokes, quizzing friends.
Week 6
Project Demo Day 🎉
Students present their AI project to the class, explain their design choices, and receive a certificate of completion. Parents are invited!

FAQs

Got questions?
We've got answers.

Does my child need to know coding? +
Not at all. We start from zero. The K–5 modules use zero coding — just games and discussions. Coding is introduced gradually for older students using simple block-based tools.
What devices are needed? +
A single tablet or Chromebook is enough. Many of our activities are completely offline — we use printed cards, role-play, and group games. Students can also use smartphones for some demos.
How long is each session? +
Each lesson is 45 minutes. The 6-week module has 3 lessons per week — fitting easily into a school timetable or as an after-school club.
Is this program safe and age-appropriate? +
Yes. We follow UNESCO and AI4K12 safety guidelines. No personal data is collected from students. All online tools are browser-based and do not require accounts. Ethics and digital citizenship are built into every module.
What does my child receive at the end? +
A completion certificate, their AI project files to keep, and a portfolio write-up they can use for school admissions or competitions. High school students build a project suitable for science fairs.
How much does it cost? +
Contact us on WhatsApp for current pricing and scholarship options. Pilot cohort spots may be subsidised. We believe no child should miss out because of cost.
