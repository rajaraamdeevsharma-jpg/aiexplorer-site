Step 3 — Find Your Targets

How to Find
Principals on LinkedIn.

Use LinkedIn's free search strategically. You can find 50–100 qualified contacts per day without Premium.

1

Basic People Search

Go to LinkedIn search bar → click "People" filter → type your search query. Filter by location: "Haryana" or your specific district.

"Principal" "school" Hisar
"Vice Principal" CBSE Haryana
"Academic coordinator" school India
2

School Page Search

Search the school name as a Company → go to their LinkedIn page → click "See all employees" → filter by "Principal", "Head", "Coordinator". This is more targeted than a general people search.

"Delhi Public School" Hisar employees
"DAV School" Haryana principal
3

LinkedIn Alumni Tool (Underused)

Go to any education-focused university page (like Kurukshetra University or MDU Hisar) → click "Alumni" → filter alumni by "Education" industry + your city. These are trained educators in your district.

4

Groups (Free — No Premium Needed)

Search LinkedIn Groups: "CBSE School Principals India", "Indian Educators Network", "School Leadership India". Join these groups → post your content → connect with active members who comment.

Step 4 — What to Say

Word-for-Word
Message Templates.

4 templates for every stage — connection request, first message, follow-up, and cold InMail. Copy. Personalise. Send.

Connection Request Note

Purpose: Get accepted · 300 character limit
Template A — For Principals
Hi [Name], I run an AI education program for K–12 students in Hisar — aligned to UNESCO and AI4K12 frameworks. I'd love to connect and share what our students are building. Would be great to have you in my network.
Template B — For CS Teachers
Hi [Name], I see you're a CS/IT teacher at [School] — I run an AI Explorer program for K–12 students and offer free teacher certification as part of our school partnership model. Would love to connect and share more.

Key rule: Never pitch in the connection request. The goal is ONLY to get accepted. Keep it warm, credible, curious. Mention something specific about them or their school to show you're not copy-pasting to everyone.

⚠️ LinkedIn limits: Free accounts can send ~20 connection requests per week. Don't blow them all on cold targets — spend 30% on warm contacts (people who engaged with your posts) and 70% on targeted principals.

First Message (After Connected)

Send within 24 hours of connection accepted · Keep under 150 words
Template — Principal / Academic Head
Hi [Name], Thank you for connecting! I wanted to reach out because we've just launched AI Explorer — a K–12 AI education program aligned to UNESCO and AI4K12 frameworks — and I'm looking to partner with forward-thinking schools in Hisar. Our students build real AI projects: image classifiers, chatbots, and ethical AI analyses. No expensive equipment needed — works on any device, even smartphones. We're offering a free pilot module (6 lessons, no commitment) to our first partner schools this term. Would you be open to a 20-minute call or screen share to see what our students are building? Happy to send our educator overview first if that's easier. Best, Raja Ram Dev Sharma

What makes this work: (1) It's short — principals are busy. (2) "Free pilot" removes financial risk. (3) "No commitment" removes pressure. (4) It offers two options (call OR overview doc) — giving them an easy low-effort way to say yes.

Template — CS / IT Teacher (Internal Champion Route)
Hi [Name], Thanks for connecting! I run AI Explorer — a K–12 AI education program — and noticed you're teaching CS/IT at [School]. I'd love your perspective on something. We've built a curriculum where Grade 5 students train image classifiers, Grade 8 students build chatbots, and Grade 11 students write Python and study AI ethics — all with free browser-based tools. As part of our school partnership model, we offer the classroom teacher free AI literacy certification (Flint + Google ISTE-backed). Would this be something worth exploring for your students? Happy to send a 2-page overview or jump on a quick call. Raja Ram Dev Sharma

Why target teachers first: A CS teacher who loves your program will walk into the principal's office and say "we should do this." That internal recommendation is 10× more powerful than a cold outreach from you.

Follow-Up Messages

If no reply after 5–7 days · Maximum 2 follow-ups then stop
Follow-Up 1 — Add Value (Day 7)
Hi [Name], Just following up on my message from last week about AI Explorer. I thought you might find this interesting — I've put together a short overview of what our students build in their first 6 weeks: [link to your educator profile HTML page]. No pressure at all — just thought it might be useful context if you're thinking about AI education for your students. If now isn't the right time, I completely understand. Happy to reconnect in the next term. Raja Ram Dev Sharma
Follow-Up 2 — Final Nudge (Day 14)
Hi [Name], Last message from me on this — I don't want to crowd your inbox! We're closing our pilot school applications for this term on Book via WhatsApp. If Your School might be interested in a free 6-lesson AI pilot for your students, I'd love to make it happen. If not — absolutely no problem. I'll reach back out next term. Either way, best of luck with the rest of the school year. Raja Ram Dev Sharma

The "Last message" technique: Telling someone it's your final message triggers reciprocity — people feel they owe you a response, even if it's a no. A no is still data. A no now is often a yes next term.

⚠️ Hard rule: Maximum 2 follow-ups. If someone doesn't reply after 3 messages total, move on. Chasing further damages your reputation and risks being reported as spam on LinkedIn. Mark them for re-contact next term.

Cold InMail (LinkedIn Premium)

For 2nd/3rd degree connections you can't reach otherwise · Use sparingly

Students build real projects — image classifiers, chatbots, and ethical AI analyses — using free browser-based tools that work on any device. InMail best practices: (1) Subject line is everything — name the school specifically. (2) Lead with what you're GIVING (free pilot) not what you want. (3) End with a specific, low-friction ask (20 min call). (4) Include your WhatsApp — many principals prefer it over email.

Step 6 — Handle Objections

What Principals
Say & How to Reply.

Every principal will raise one of these 5 objections. Tap to see exactly how to respond.

"We don't have the budget for extra programs right now."
+

This is the #1 objection. They assume it costs money. Reframe immediately.

"Completely understood — which is exactly why I lead with a free 6-lesson pilot for partner schools. No budget required, no commitment to continue. I cover the lesson materials and teacher training at no cost. If your students love it and you want to expand, we can talk about options at that point. Would a free pilot still be a problem?"
"Our teachers don't have the technical background to teach AI."
+

This is actually an opportunity, not a barrier. Flip it.

"That's exactly why we include free AI literacy certification for the classroom teacher as part of every partnership. Our teachers complete a 4-hour online course (free, from Flint and Google) before the program starts. No technical background needed — if they can use a smartphone, they can run our lessons. We've trained teachers with zero tech background and they're now our best advocates."
"Our students need to focus on board exams, not extra subjects."
+

This is a priority objection. Show relevance to their core goals.

"Absolutely — board prep comes first. Our program is designed to complement, not compete with board preparation. Most schools run it as an after-school club, a free period, or a Saturday session. It's also worth noting that CBSE and NEP 2020 now include AI as a recommended subject area — so this actually supports your curriculum compliance, not adds to the burden."
"We already have a computer science program."
+

Don't compete — show the gap your program fills.

"That's great — CS and AI are complementary but different. Most CS programs teach programming syntax and software concepts. Our AI program teaches students to train machine learning models, understand algorithmic bias, and design ethical AI systems. These are skills that even most CS graduates don't have yet. Many of our best partner schools already have strong CS programs — our module plugs into it rather than replacing it."
"Send me more information and I'll think about it."
+

This is a soft no — or a delay tactic. Don't just send a PDF and wait. Move them forward.

"Of course — I'll send our educator overview right now. [Send the educator profile HTML page link.] While I have you — would it make sense to do a quick 20-minute screen share so I can show you what students actually build? I find it's much clearer than a document. I have Tuesday at 4pm or Thursday at 11am free — does either work?"
